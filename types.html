Types of Ocean Animals

Fish

Fish are cold-blooded animals that live in water, breathe through gills, and usually have fins and scales.

Mammals

Marine mammals, like whales and dolphins, are warm-blooded, breathe air, and nurse their young with milk.

Reptiles

Sea reptiles, such as sea turtles, have lungs, lay eggs, and are adapted to life in the ocean.

Invertebrates

Invertebrates like squid and octopus have no backbone and come in many shapes and sizes.

Ocean Animal Classification

Ocean animals are classified into major groups based on their body structure, biology, and evolutionary history. Each group has unique adaptations that help them survive in marine environments, from shallow coral reefs to the deep sea.
